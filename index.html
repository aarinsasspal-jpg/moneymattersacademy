Our Purpose

The gap is real, and widest where it hurts most.

Most young people reach adulthood with no financial education, and the consequences follow them for decades. That is the gap Money Matters Academy exists to close.

What You Learn

Real money skills, taught in plain terms.

  • How to budget. Play the $5,000 budgeting challenge using real Southern California costs.
  • Saving vs. investing. See how compound interest grows money over time.
  • How the stock market works. Stocks, bonds, index funds, and what moves prices.
  • Building a portfolio. Practice investing a hypothetical $1,000 and explain your choices.
The Curriculum

Three modules, built from scratch.

Each module pairs a presentation with a hands-on worksheet built around real numbers.

MODULE 01

Core Concepts

Income, expenses, savings, investing, interest, and simple vs. compound growth, all in plain language.

Income vs. expenses · Checking vs. savings · Saving vs. investing · Compound interest
MODULE 02

Budgeting

The $5,000 Budgeting Challenge using real Southern California cost-of-living data. Needs, wants, and what is left over.

Why budgeting matters · 50/30/20 · Needs vs. wants · Saving for goals
MODULE 03

Investing Basics

The four types of investments, plus a worksheet where students allocate $1,000 and justify each choice.

Why markets move · Risk vs. reward · Diversification · Stocks, bonds, funds, crypto

About the Founder

Aarin Sasspal

Founder and Student Director

I started Money Matters Academy after watching classmates leave middle school with no idea what a budget looked like, what compound interest did, or why starting early mattered.

Most schools do not teach this, and the ones that do teach it too late. So I built the curriculum I wish I had, and made it free for any student, in any classroom. We focus on Title I schools, where the gap is widest, ahead of California's AB 2927 personal finance mandate.

Questions

Frequently asked questions.

How much does it cost?

Nothing. Every Money Matters Academy session and resource is completely free.

Who is it for?

Students in grades 5 to 12. The core curriculum is designed for middle schoolers, but high schoolers are welcome.

Are sessions online or in person?

Both. We run virtual workshops and in-person sessions at schools and community centers.

Do I need anything to attend?

Just something to write with.

Can you come to my school?

Yes. Use the Request a Workshop form and we will reach out to set a date.

Can I start a chapter at my school?

Yes. We provide the full curriculum, slides, worksheets, and onboarding support so you can teach from day one.
