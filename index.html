California AB 2927

California now requires every high school to offer a semester of personal finance by 2027-28, with the course becoming a graduation requirement for the class of 2030-31. We’re building curriculum for students who can’t wait for the law to catch up.

About

A student initiative,
built for students.

Money Matters Academy was founded in 2026 to close a gap most schools still aren’t closing: teaching young people how money actually works before they have to use it. Clear curriculum. Real scenarios. No jargon. No fees.

We focus on middle schoolers because that’s when financial habits begin to form, and we prioritize Title I schools because that’s where the gap is widest.

A
Aarin Sasspal
Founder & Student Director

I started Money Matters Academy because I watched friends and classmates graduate middle school with no idea what a budget looked like, what compound interest actually did, or why starting early mattered.

Most schools don’t teach this. The ones that do often teach it too late. So I built the curriculum I wish I’d had, and I’m making it free for any student, any classroom, anywhere.

The Curriculum

Three modules,
built from scratch.

Designed for grades 6–8. Each module pairs a visual presentation with a hands-on worksheet built around real numbers from real life.

Module 01

Core Concepts

Start with the language of money. Income, expenses, savings, investing, interest, and the difference between simple and compound growth, all explained in plain terms.

Topics: Income vs. expenses · Checking vs. savings · Saving vs. investing · Simple vs. compound interest
Module 02

Budgeting

The $5,000 Budgeting Challenge is a hands-on exercise using real Southern California cost-of-living data. Students work through needs, wants, and what’s actually left over.

Topics: Why budgeting matters · 50/30/20 framework · Needs vs. wants · Saving for big goals
Module 03

Investing Basics

The four types of investments (stocks, bonds, index funds, cryptocurrency), plus a portfolio-building worksheet where students allocate $1,000 and justify each choice.

Topics: Why markets move · Risk vs. reward · Diversification · Conservative vs. risky bets

Our Purpose

The problem is real and urgent

Most young people enter adulthood with zero financial education. The consequences follow them for decades. That is exactly why Money Matters Academy exists.
